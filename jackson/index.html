About Us

Who Am I?

I always knew that I wanted to work in the art world. After completing an art foundation course in Edinburgh I realised that I wanted to turn away from subjective fine art in order to focus on objective functional design. This was confirmed in me after working at various incarnations of the art world. I therefore enrolled at GA’s immersive UX design course and have since discovered a huge love and affinity for it. I want to pursue this in a professional capacity, and my dream job would be as a UX designer in a friendly and creative cross-functional team, building something that positively impacts people’s lives.

Education

Stanford University | Computer Science

CS101 is a self-paced course that teaches the essential ideas of Computer Science for a zero-prior-experience audience.

    Topics:

  • The nature of computers and code, what they can and cannot do
  • How computer hardware works: chips, cpu, memory, disk
  • Necessary jargon: bits, bytes, megabytes, gigabytes
  • How software works: what is a program, what is "running"
  • How digital images work
  • Computer code: loops and logic
  • Big ideas: abstraction, logic, bugs
  • How structured data works
  • How the internet works: ip address, routing, ethernet, wi-fi
  • Computer security: viruses, trojans, and passwords, oh my!
  • Analog vs. digital
  • Digital media, images, sounds, video, compression.

General Assembly | User Experience Design

Intensive 3 month industry-led course covering the full range of UX design skills and processes including research, design, prototyping, testing and iteration. We would start the day with a morning of lectures and activities. After lunch we would then have workshop time where would practice our new skills, work on our projects, and receive extra help from the instructors.

    Concept project 1: App Blocker

    In the one-week concept project, I was challenged with creating a mobile app for my classmate to aid them in their day to day lives. As Howie that felt he was on his phone too much I created an app blocker that was personalised to him. This project was about rapid prototyping and creating an interactive prototype of designs based on user research.

    Concept project 2: Take Note Stationery

    In weeks two and three we were tasked with creating a mid-fi prototype of an e-commerce website for a Stationery shop. We used the lean UX model and Double Diamond to achieve this.

    Concept project 3: Unplug

    In fourth week, we went back to our projects from week one to work on the graphic design, and so developed our fundamental UI skills.

    Concept project 4: Netflix Originals Microsite

    During a two-week concept project, my team and I were asked to develop a microsite for Netflix originals. We created a high fidelity responsive prototype.

    Client project: Stay Nimble

    For the final project at GA, my group and I worked for Stay Nimble, a grass roots social enterprise that help people change careers. As they had recently launched their website, we helped redesign and streamline their homepage, onboarding process and product page so it was more user friendly and thus in better stead to attract a larger client base.

Experience

A few of the internships I have had

Art Critic for Arteviste

In my second year of university I worked for Arteviste: a contemporary online platform passionate about making arts and culture accessible to the digital generation. I wrote and edited exhibition reviews, artist interviews and commentaries on the art scenes in London, Paris and New York. In this way I was able to gain valuable insight into the inner workings of a successful entrepreneurial startup, and was taught how to manipulate and design the Arteviste website.

Design Team for the University of Cambridge Trinity Hall May Ball

As Artist in Residence for Trinity Hall’s May Ball - the college’s largest event of the year, where tickets sell out in seconds - I painted huge murals for the atrium, oversaw the production of artwork from the rest of the design team, and was on site to ensure the event ran smoothly and according to plan. The small window of time we had to curate the event meant good time management was required.

The Gagosian Gallery

During this 5 month long paid internship in New York City, I worked across all the departments of the Gagosian, thus gaining invaluable insight into the machinations of the world famous modern art gallery.

Studio Lotus

Studio Lotus is a multi-disciplinary design practice whose work seamlessly weaves interior and exterior spaces, from large architectural ideas to the smallest of furniture details. Whilst working here, I learnt to look at design through the lens of cultural, social and environmental impact, and was taught how to intergrate localized skills and resources with state of the art materials and technologies.

The Frick Collection

In the course of this one month internship for the Frick Collection’s Curatorial Department I catalogued upcoming exhibitions and kept the archives up to date. I was able to meet and shadow a wide range of curators and so gained a first hand unique view of museum culture.
